The Foundry

42-38 Ninth Street

Long Island City, NY 11101

One day a Sam was born.

Dragging herself from the primordial soup from which The Virgin God birthed her, she felt a pit in her stomach. Hunger.

She absorbed what nutrients the earth could offer her infant form, but the dirt could not satiate her. Her tummy's quest was greater.

"Where is the cake! What happened to the cake!" The Sam cried out. She longed for the delicacies of her past life in Cake World.

God answered her prayer and granted her hands and a few more brain cells.

The Sam went on to craft the best cakes in the land, consumed by all creatures.

But while the creatures abrorbed the cake's nutrients, they ate indiscriminantly. They had no taste.

Sad Sam wished for a buddy who could appreciate the art of cakemaking.

That night she made the most unique cake the world had ever known.

A Justin was born.

Sugar, flower, goat hooves, caterpillar fuzz, and monkeys milk.

She taught him the abcs of cake making, but he did not possess the dexterity from his caterpillar vessel and goat hooves.

"You are no prodigy," The Sam huffed.

Cake making ceased while The Justin gorged himself in the discarded mountains of red velvet and vanilla icing.

After the mountain was fully consumed, The Justin writhed in dual pains of tummyache and withdrawl.

His cries were so pitiful that The Sam came to his aid.

"Cake or death," he wheezed.

The Sam realized The Justin was the perfect audience for her cakes all along.
